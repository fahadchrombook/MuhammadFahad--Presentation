Resolution to Revolution

How 1940–1947 Created Hope and Fear in South Asia

Written By: MuhammadFahad
Teacher: Ayesha Ammar
Date: 4/18/2026

Driving Question

The events between 1940 and 1947 shaped South Asia’s future in a very powerful way. People felt hope because independence from British rule seemed close. At the same time, fear increased because different groups had different ideas about how the country should be governed. As political talks failed and tensions increased, uncertainty spread among people about safety, unity, and the future of their homeland.

Lahore Resolution (1940)

This resolution gave Muslims hope for a separate homeland where they could live freely and protect their identity, religion, and rights. However, it also created fear among other communities who worried that India would be divided. It was the beginning of stronger political separation.

Cripps Mission (1942)

The British offered future independence, which created hope among Indians. However, since it did not give immediate freedom, it was rejected. This increased frustration and fear that independence might never come peacefully.

Simla Conference (1945)

This conference failed because leaders could not agree on how power should be shared. This failure increased fear and showed that political unity was breaking down.

Partition (1947)

India was divided into two countries. This created hope for independence but also caused massive migration and violence. Millions suffered, making this moment both joyful and tragic.
